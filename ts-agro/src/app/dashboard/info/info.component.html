Formed as the 29th State of India, Telangana came into being on the 2nd of June, 2014. The State has an area of 1,12,077 sq. Km. and has a population of 3,50,03,674 (2011 Census). The Telangana region was part of the Hyderabad state from 17th September 1948 to 1st November 1956, until it was merged with Andhra State to form Andhra Pradesh.

Telangana has a semi-arid to arid climate with hot summers and dry winters. The state receives most of its rainfall during the monsoon season, which is from June to September. The average annual rainfall in the state is around 900 mm.

The soils in Telangana are predominantly red sandy loams, red loams, and clay loams. The state also has black cotton soils in some areas. The soils in Telangana are generally fertile, but there are areas where the soil is saline and alkaline.

Rice, cotton, maize, jowar, bajra, red gram, green gram, black gram, and groundnut are the major crops grown in Telangana. Other important crops include sugarcane, tobacco, chillies, turmeric, and mango.

Telangana has a well-developed irrigation system with around 18 major, medium, and minor irrigation projects. The state has a total of 14,394 tanks and ponds, which are the main source of irrigation for agriculture in the state.

The state has several agricultural research institutions, including the Professor Jayashankar Telangana State Agricultural University and the National Academy of Agricultural Research Management. These institutions conduct research and provide training to farmers to help improve agricultural productivity in the state.
